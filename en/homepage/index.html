FAQ

Find answers to the most frequently asked questions about settling in Geneva and your property search.

Why use N20 Immobilier for your property search in Geneva?
+

N20 Immobilier specialises in relocation in Geneva and has deep knowledge of the local market, neighbourhoods, and Swiss-specific requirements (applications, permits, agency practices). You save considerable time and avoid the common mistakes of searching alone in a very competitive market. Our team guides you every step of the way: defining your needs, shortlisting properties, organising viewings, preparing your file, and following through to key handover.

What is a relocation agency in Geneva?
+

A relocation agency like N20 Immobilier supports you in your move to Geneva: apartment search, organising viewings, preparing the rental application, administrative procedures (residence permit, SIG energy contracts, insurance) and integration assistance. Unlike a standard real estate agency, we manage the entire installation process to save you time and avoid costly mistakes.

Which Geneva neighbourhoods do you search?
+

N20 Immobilier covers the entire canton of Geneva: city centre, Eaux-Vives, Carouge, Champel, Grand-Saconnex, Meyrin, Lancy, Vernier, as well as the French border communes (Ferney-Voltaire, Saint-Genis-Pouilly, Annemasse). We tailor the search to your priorities: international schools, proximity to your workplace, budget, neighbourhood atmosphere.

Do you assist expats and international civil servants?
+

Yes, this is one of our core specialisations. We regularly assist expats on assignment, civil servants from international organisations (UN, WHO, ICRC, WTO) and their families. We understand the specific constraints: tight arrival timelines, high-standard accommodation requirements, proximity to international schools, and accompanying partner support.

What documents are needed to rent an apartment in Geneva?
+

To build a strong rental application in Geneva, you will need: a valid identity document or passport, a residence permit (or proof of application in progress), your last 3 pay slips or employment contract, an extract from the debt enforcement register. N20 supports you in building your application with complete peace of mind.

Is it possible to live in France and work in Geneva?
+

Absolutely. Thousands of cross-border commuters make this choice every day. Living in Ferney-Voltaire, Saint-Genis-Pouilly or Annemasse offers more affordable rents while working in Geneva. N20 can advise you on the best communes based on your workplace, available transport links (Léman Express, TPG, motorway) and your budget.
